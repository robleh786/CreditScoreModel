Credit Smart Glossary

Credit Mix

The variety of credit types that you have, such as credit cards, mortgages, and car loans. A diverse credit mix can improve your credit score.

EMI (Equated Monthly Installment)

A fixed payment amount made by a borrower to a lender at a specified date each calendar month. EMIs are used to pay off both interest and principal each month so that over a specified number of years, the loan is paid off in full.

Credit Utilization Ratio

The ratio of your current total debt to your total available credit. This ratio helps determine your credit score and is a key indicator of your credit health.

RePayment status

Repayment status indicates if a user paid their credit card bill on time or with a delay. Recorded monthly (PAY_0 to PAY_6), values range from -1 (paid on time) to 9 (nine or more months overdue). This helps assess creditworthiness.

Bill amount

The BILL_AMT variables represent the billed amount on a client's credit card statement for six consecutive months. The values, recorded as BILL_AMT1 to BILL_AMT6, show the outstanding balance on the card at the end of each month. This helps evaluate the client's credit usage and financial behavior.
